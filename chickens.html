What We Need

Chickens

  • Total: 45 chickens (accounting for 25% failure to thrive rate)
  • Target: 3 chickens per person for 10-12 people

Breeds & Costs

  • Blue Austrolorp: 15 × $7/female = $105
  • Delawares: 15 × $10/female = $150
  • Black Copper Marans: 15 × $10/female = $150
  • Total Chickens: $405

Infrastructure

  • Brooding Boxes: 2 boxes, 6' × 7.5' each (45 sq ft per box)
  • Electric Mesh Fencing: 164' kit for $199 at Tractor Supply
  • Total Space: 1000 square feet needed
  • Mobile Chicken Coop: 6' × 10' (holds 15-50 chickens)
  • Trailer: 6' × 10'-12 for $1600

Feed & Nutrition

  • 8 weeks feed: 10 × 50lb bags = 500lbs at $0.50/lb = $250
  • Up to 18 weeks feed: 30 × 50lb bags = 1500lbs at $0.50/lb = $750
  • Poultry grit: $8
  • Oyster shell (18-20 weeks): 5 lbs for $8 (lasts ~3 months)
  • Feed Storage Totes: $50

Housing & Supplies

  • Shavings: 5 × 8 cubic feet bags at $8.10 each = $40.50
  • Nesting boxes: 12 boxes (free DIY or $180 purchased at $15 each)
  • Brooding: 2 × 105L totes at $15 each = $30
  • Housing: IBC totes cut in half (free) until they go outside

Heating & Equipment

  • Heat lamps: 2 at $15 each = $30
  • Brooder thermometers: 2 at $7 each = $14
  • Spare bulbs: $30
  • Chicken waterer & feeders: $60
  • 120V ventilation fan: $80

Health & Safety

  • Hardware cloth: 100' × 2' roll for $80
  • Cleaning supplies: $30
  • Tools: Shovel, scoop, rake, gloves
  • Dust bath materials: Sawmill (free)
  • First aid kit: Covered
  • Waste disposal: Chicken manure as fertilizer for garden

Construction & Features

  • Building materials for coop: $0 (already have)
  • Roosting bars: TBD (may construct ourselves, $35-70 each if purchased, need 8-10 bars for 45 chickens)
  • Ramp for mobile coop: Covered
  • Wheelbarrow: Covered

Seasonal Protection

  • Winter protection: Insulation, windbreaks (TBD)
  • Summer cooling: TBD
  • Power: Backup power not needed (power not an issue)

Cost Summary

  • Chickens: $405
  • Infrastructure: $1,799 (fencing $199 + trailer $1600)
  • Feed: $1,008 (first 18 weeks)
  • Supplies: $70.50 (shavings + grit + oyster shell)
  • Equipment: $214 (heating + thermometers + bulbs + waterers/feeders)

Ongoing Costs (After 18 Weeks)

Monthly Costs

  • Layer feed: ~$150-200 (for 45 laying hens)
  • Shavings: ~$20-30 (monthly replacement)
  • Grit: ~$5-10 (as needed)
  • Oyster shell: ~$3 (already budgeted for 3 months)
  • Egg cartons: $10-20 (if selling eggs)
  • Health supplies: $5-10 (preventative care)

Monthly Total: ~$193-273

Annual Costs

  • Feed: ~$2,400 (yearly total)
  • Shavings: ~$240-360
  • Grit: ~$60-120
  • Oyster shell: ~$32 (4 bags per year)
  • Egg cartons: ~$120-240
  • Health supplies: ~$60-120
  • Veterinary fund: ~$200-300 (emergency/annual check-ups)
  • Coop repairs/maintenance: ~$100-200

Annual Total: ~$3,212-3,772

Potential Income

  • Egg production: 45 hens ÷ 10 hens = 4.5 groups × 5-7 eggs/day = 22.5-31.5 eggs/day
  • Annual production: 22.5-31.5 × 365 = 8,213-11,498 eggs/year
  • Egg sales: 684-958 dozen × $3-5/dozen = $2,052-4,790 potential income

Net annual result: Loss of $1,720 to profit of $1,018 after ongoing costs

Family Food Security Analysis

Egg Production for Family

  • Family size: 12 people
  • Egg needs: 10 eggs per person per week = 120 eggs/week
  • Weekly production: 22.5-31.5 eggs/day = 157-220 eggs/week
  • Surplus: 37-100 extra eggs per week for sharing/trading

Cost Comparison

  • Store-bought organic eggs: $5-6/dozen
  • Your cost per dozen: ~$3-4 (including all costs)
  • Weekly savings: $15-25 on grocery bills
  • Annual savings: $780-1,300

Egg Production & Potential Extras

  • Family needs: 120 eggs/week (10 eggs × 12 people)
  • Weekly production: 157-220 eggs/week
  • Weekly extras: 37-100 eggs/week
  • Annual extras: 1,924-5,200 eggs/year
  • Extras in dozens: 160-433 dozen/year

Potential Profit from Extras

  • If sell extras at $3/dozen: $480-1,299/year
  • If sell extras at $4/dozen: $640-1,732/year
  • If sell extras at $5/dozen: $800-2,165/year

Net Financial Impact (Family + Extras)

  • Annual operating costs: $3,212-3,772
  • Value of family eggs: 6,240 eggs/year = 520 dozen = $1,560-3,120 (store price)
  • Plus extra sales income: $480-2,165
  • Total annual value: $2,040-5,285
  • Net annual result: Loss of $1,172 to profit of $1,513

Break-Even Timeline

  • Setup cost: $3,736.50
  • Years to break even: 2.5-6 years (depending on extra egg sales)
  • Best case: Break even in 2.5 years selling extras at $5/dozen
  • Worst case: Break even in 6 years with minimal extra sales

Benefits Beyond Cost

  • Fresh, healthy eggs from well-cared-for chickens
  • Food independence from supply chain issues
  • Educational experience for family
  • Garden fertilizer from chicken waste
  • Connection to food source

Wish List

Peacocks

  • Considerations: More space required, louder, can be flighty, need secure roosting
  • Advantages: Beautiful ornamental birds, natural pest control, good watchdogs, long lifespan (15-20 years)
  • Requirements: 6-8 foot fencing, more expensive, may wander, predator protection needed
